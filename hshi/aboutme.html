About Me

As a computational physicist, I use Auxiliary Field Quantum Monte Carlo (AFQMC) methods to study strongly correlated systems. I have developed a variety of new AFQMC methods with my adviser Shiwei Zhang:  1. Add symmetry in both trial wave function and projector to AFQMC  2. Develop the release constraint method  3. Use force-bias method to improve the efficiency in Metropolis  4. Solve the infinite variance problem.  I used these developments to study two dimensional Fermi gas and Hubbard model, these works have been served as benchmark for these systems. Meanwhile, I'm also working on the spin orbit coupling and dynamic information.

Besides AFQMC methods, I have worked on Exact Diagonalization for the topological phase transition in interacting Haldane model, have some reasearch experience  in Dynamic Mean Field Theory, Continuous Time Quantum Monte Carlo and Density Matrix Renormalization Group. You can find my publication list here.

I'm familiar with a few programming languages such as Fortran, C++, Python and Mathematica, use Cmake and Git for developing the code. Since my research is associated with massive palatalization, MPI and OpenMP are used a lot for high performance computing.  For writing paper, I use Latex to reach publishable quality.

Some C++ librarys developed during my sparse time are listed under the Programs, I hope they will be useful to your work. Enjoy!

I received B.S. in physics from Nanjing University in China,  also studied Computational Physics in Renmin University.

I'm fast in learning things, interested in new physics and technology.  If you like my website or my work, feel free to contact me through email (click here) or following links.
